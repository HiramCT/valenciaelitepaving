Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from our residential, commercial and municipal clients.

A properly installed asphalt driveway in Ontario typically lasts 20–30 years with regular maintenance including sealcoating every 3–5 years and prompt crack repair. Freeze-thaw cycles are the primary stressor — a correct base depth and drainage system are essential.

The optimal paving season is late spring through early fall (May–October), when temperatures stay consistently above 10°C. Cold temperatures affect hot-mix asphalt compaction and adhesion. Emergency cold-patch repairs can be performed year-round.

Residential driveway paving in Ontario typically ranges from $3.50–$7.00/sq ft for resurfacing and $6.00–$12.00/sq ft for full-depth new installation depending on gravel base requirements, accessibility, site conditions and current asphalt pricing. Commercial and municipal rates vary by scope. We provide detailed written estimates at no obligation.

Yes. All work is covered by a detailed written quotation specifying scope, materials, thickness, timeline and pricing. We do not proceed with any work without a signed contract. Commercial and municipal work also includes applicable bonds and insurance certificates.

New hot-mix asphalt is typically safe for light vehicle traffic within 24–48 hours under warm conditions. Full cure takes 6–12 months. We advise against sharp turns, heavy trucks, jacks, and kickstands during the first full summer season. Sealcoating should wait a minimum of 12 months after new paving.

Yes. Valencia's Elite Paving is fully licensed and insured to operate across the Greater Toronto Area and Ontario.

Sealcoating is a protective emulsion coating applied to asphalt surfaces every 3–5 years. It seals micro-oxidation cracks, protects against fuel and oil spill damage, UV degradation, and water infiltration — the leading cause of pothole formation in Ontario's frost-prone climate. It is the most cost-effective way to extend pavement life.
